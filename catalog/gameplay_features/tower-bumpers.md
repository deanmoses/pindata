A type of [[gameplayfeature:passive-bumpers]] consisting of a round cylindrical plastic housing that contains a rebound switch, encircled by a rubber ring. The ball strikes the rubber ring and bounces away while the internal switch registers the hit and awards points — but unlike [[gameplayfeature:pop-bumpers]], no solenoid fires to kick the ball. [[manufacturer:chicago-coin]] introduced the tower bumper on *[[title:sally]]* (1948), and the design became one of the more distinctive passive bumper variants of the late 1940s and 1950s. The tall, cylindrical profile gave the mechanism its name and made it a visible landmark on the playfield.
