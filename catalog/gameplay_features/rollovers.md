name: Rollovers

Switches embedded in or just above the playfield surface that register the ball's passage without obstructing its path. Unlike [[gameplayfeature:bumpers]] or [[gameplayfeature:drop-targets]], which the ball strikes head-on, rollovers are activated from above — the ball rolls over a contact point, depresses a mechanism, and closes a switch beneath. Designers place them in lanes, return paths, and scoring zones to track ball movement and advance objectives. The rollover family includes several variants: [[gameplayfeature:rollover-buttons]], which sit slightly raised; flush-mounted [[gameplayfeature:star-rollovers]]; the ornamental [[gameplayfeature:butterfly-rollovers]]; and hybrid [[gameplayfeature:rollover-drop-targets]] that retract when triggered.
