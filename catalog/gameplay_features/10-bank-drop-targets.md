A [[gameplay-feature:bank-drop-targets]] configuration with ten [[gameplay-feature:drop-targets]] in a row — the largest standard bank size cataloged by IPDB. Almost exclusively a [[manufacturer:gottlieb]] feature, the ten-bank appeared on approximately 20 machines, primarily during the early-to-mid 1970s. *[[title:jack-in-the-box-jumping-jack]]* (1973) was the highest-production example. The ten-target row spanned the full width of the playfield, creating a formidable completion challenge that demanded consistent accuracy across every shot — a hallmark of Gottlieb's design philosophy during the [[technology-generation:electromechanical]] era.
