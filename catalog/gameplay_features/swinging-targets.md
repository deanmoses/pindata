A motor-driven target that moves constantly from side to side across the playfield, requiring the player to time their shot to hit a moving object. Unlike stationary [[gameplay-feature:standup-targets]] or [[gameplay-feature:drop-targets]], swinging targets introduce a dynamic timing element — the ball must arrive at the right moment in the target's arc to register a hit. They appeared on approximately 31 IPDB-cataloged machines, primarily during the [[technology-generation:electromechanical]] era, and represent one of pinball's attempts to add arcade-style moving-target challenges to the playfield.
