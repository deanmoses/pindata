An early [[gameplay-feature:flippers]] mechanism that operates through purely mechanical linkage rather than solenoid power. The player presses a button that physically connects to the flipper bat through levers and springs, with no electrical solenoid to amplify the force. Mechanical flippers predate the solenoid-driven designs that became standard on [[technology-generation:electromechanical]] machines, and they delivered a weaker, less consistent stroke. The transition from mechanical to solenoid-powered flippers in the late 1940s and early 1950s gave players the crisp, powerful flipper response that made precise aiming possible and transformed pinball into a game of directed skill.
