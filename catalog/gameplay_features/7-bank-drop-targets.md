A [[gameplayfeature:bank-drop-targets]] configuration with seven [[gameplayfeature:drop-targets]] in a row. Longer than the typical three-to-five bank, the seven-bank presented a demanding completion challenge — often themed around pool or billiard pockets. IPDB catalogs approximately 38 machines featuring this arrangement. [[manufacturer:gottlieb]] favored it heavily, with *[[title:hot-shot]]* (1973) and *[[title:eight-ball-deluxe]]* (1981) among the best-known examples. The extended target row required precise shooting across the full width of the playfield, rewarding players who could methodically clear every target in the sequence.
