The most common form of [[gameplayfeature:standup-targets]] — a simple fixed-position target that remains in place when struck and does not move, kick back, or retract. A flat plate mounted on a leaf switch, the stationary standup target registers a hit through deflection alone and returns to its resting position by spring tension. Distinguished from active variants like [[gameplayfeature:kicking-targets]], [[gameplayfeature:vari-targets]], and [[gameplayfeature:swinging-targets]], the stationary standup target is the default when no qualifying adjective is applied. Its mechanical simplicity and compact footprint make it the most widely deployed target type in pinball history.
