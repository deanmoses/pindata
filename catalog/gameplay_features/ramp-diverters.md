A type of [[gameplay-feature:diverters]] built into or positioned at ramp junctions to route the ball between different ramp exits. Rather than redirecting the ball on the open playfield, a ramp diverter operates within the enclosed ramp structure itself, switching a gate or flap to send the ball down one return path instead of another. [[manufacturer:capcom]]'s *[[title:big-bang-bar]]* (1996) used ramp diverters, and [[manufacturer:stern-pinball]] employed them on titles such as *[[title:ripleys-believe-it-or-not]]* (2004) and *[[title:rush]]* (2022). They give designers a compact way to multiply the outcomes of a single ramp shot without adding new playfield real estate.
