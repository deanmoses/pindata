A type of [[gameplayfeature:standup-targets]] with a dual-switch design: a broad outer ring surrounds a small center hole through which a narrow pin protrudes. Hitting the outer ring scores one value, while threading the ball precisely into the center pin scores a higher reward. The bullseye target thus offers two tiers of scoring from a single target — a near miss still earns points, but a dead-center shot pays significantly more. They appeared on approximately 29 IPDB-cataloged machines and reward the kind of precise shot-making that separates casual players from skilled ones.
