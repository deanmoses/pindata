A [[gameplayfeature:drop-targets]] variant activated by the ball rolling over the target rather than striking its upright face. Instead of standing vertically in the ball's path, a rollover drop target sits flush with or slightly above the playfield surface, dropping below when the ball passes over it. This orientation allows designers to place drop-target completion challenges in lanes and pathways where a conventional upright target would block the ball's flow. [[manufacturer:gottlieb]]'s *[[title:tx-sector]]* (1988) and [[manufacturer:stern-pinball]]'s *[[title:the-walking-dead]]* (2014) both featured rollover drop targets integrated into lane designs.
