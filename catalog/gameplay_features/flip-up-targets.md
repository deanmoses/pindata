A type of [[gameplay-feature:targets]] that flip upward when struck by the ball, the mechanical opposite of [[gameplay-feature:drop-targets]]. Where a drop target retracts below the playfield surface on impact, a flip-up target rises or rotates upward from a flat resting position, popping into a visible raised state to indicate it has been hit. The upward motion provides immediate visual feedback and changes the local playfield geometry until the target resets. [[manufacturer:bally]]'s *[[title:elvira-and-the-party-monsters]]* (1989) featured flip-up targets, using the inverted-drop-target mechanic to add a distinctive shot feedback during the [[technology-generation:solid-state]] era.
