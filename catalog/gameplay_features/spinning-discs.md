A circular, motorized turntable inset into the playfield surface that spins rapidly during game play. When the ball rolls onto the disc, the rotation flings it off in an unpredictable direction, adding a chaotic element that no amount of skill can fully control. [[manufacturer:chicago-coin]] featured spinning discs as early as 1972, and the mechanism appeared on approximately 17 IPDB-cataloged machines. The spinning disc is one of pinball's most dramatic playfield elements — a visible, kinetic hazard that disrupts the ball's path and forces the player to react to whatever trajectory the spin produces.
