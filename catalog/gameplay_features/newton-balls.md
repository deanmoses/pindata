name: Newton Balls

A pinball used as an immobile intermediary in a [[gameplayfeature:captive-ball]] assembly, transferring the momentum of the ball-in-play to a movable captive ball on the other side — the same principle as a Newton's cradle desktop toy. The Newton ball itself does not move; it sits fixed in the assembly and relays kinetic energy from one ball to the next. This arrangement appeared on approximately 16 IPDB-cataloged machines and creates a distinctive feel: the player's shot stops dead on contact while the captive ball on the far side springs to life and strikes the scoring switch.
