Motor-driven [[gameplay-feature:targets]] that physically change position during gameplay, requiring the player to time their shot rather than simply aim at a fixed location. Distinct from [[gameplay-feature:swinging-targets]], which oscillate side to side on a pivot, moving targets may travel along tracks or shift in other patterns under motor power. [[manufacturer:williams]] featured a moving target on *[[title:kismet]]* (1961), one of the earliest examples. Modern implementations appear on machines like [[manufacturer:jersey-jack]]'s *[[title:dialed-in]]* (2017), where a motor-driven target adds a timing challenge that rewards patience and precision over raw shot-making.
